3. VOCABULARY DECISIONS
================================================================================

VD-001: XEN-TERM-ID Format
----------------------------
Decision: Every term has a persistent ID: XEN-TERM-{DOMAIN}-{NNNN}
Rationale: Stable identifiers independent of name changes. Domain prefix
enables namespace-based filtering (e.g., all protection terms start with
XEN-TERM-PROT-).

VD-002: 10-Domain Coverage
-----------------------------
Decision: Vocabulary covers exactly 10 engineering domains, 145+ terms.
Rationale: Matches the taxonomy domain structure from K1.1. Provides
complete coverage of power electrical engineering. 15+ terms per domain
ensures adequate depth for each area.

VD-003: Deprecated Terms Tracked
-----------------------------------
Decision: Every term records its deprecated predecessors.
Rationale: Preserves backward compatibility. When old documents use
deprecated terms, the system can resolve them to current canonical terms.
Prevents knowledge loss during terminology evolution.

VD-004: Embedding Strategy
-----------------------------
Decision: Canonical terms use `name_en + ": " + definition` as primary
embedding text; `name_fa` as secondary.
Rationale: English definitions produce higher-quality embeddings with
current models. Persian names provide cross-language retrieval support.

================================================================================
4. SYNONYM DECISIONS
================================================================================

SD-001: 68 Synonym Groups as Initial Foundation
--------------------------------------------------
Decision: 68 groups covering all major electrical engineering domains.
Rationale: Covers the most common synonym variations encountered in
engineering documents. Expandable as new synonyms are discovered.

SD-002: Context Rules for Ambiguous Synonyms
-----------------------------------------------
Decision: Synonyms that map to different canonical terms in different
contexts include explicit context_rules for disambiguation.
Example: "Power Factor" maps to cosφ in AC systems but to "Power Factor
Correction" when referring to capacitor banks.
Rationale: Prevents incorrect resolution in AI reasoning.

SD-003: Synonym → Graph Edge Design
---------------------------------------
Decision: Synonyms form EQUIVALENT_TO edges to canonical term nodes.
Rationale: Graph query can traverse synonym edges transparently.
Querying for any synonym retrieves documents tagged with the canonical
term and vice versa.

7. UNIT NORMALIZATION DECISIONS
================================================================================

UND-001: SI Compliant with Persian Formatting
------------------------------------------------
Decision: All units follow SI standards with Persian formatting rules
for display.
Rationale: SI ensures international compatibility. Persian formatting
(decimal separator, digits, RTL) ensures correct display for Iranian
users and document processing.

UND-002: Per Unit System Documentation
------------------------------------------
Decision: Dedicated Per Unit system section with base value selection
rules and common power system base values.
Rationale: Per-unit is the standard representation in power engineering.
AI services must normalize to pu for meaningful comparison.

UND-003: Comprehensive Conversion Tables
-------------------------------------------
Decision: Conversion tables for AWG↔mm², hp↔kW, °F↔°C, etc.
Rationale: Engineering documents use mixed unit systems (especially
manufacturer catalogs using AWG or hp). Automated conversion is required
for AI reasoning.

UND-004: AI Normalization Requirement
-----------------------------------------
Decision: AI services must normalize to canonical units before any
calculation or comparison.
Rationale: "12 kV" and "12000 V" must be recognized as identical. All
numerical values in the knowledge base stored in canonical SI units.

TD-001: 5-Level Hierarchy (v2)
---------------------------------
Decision: v1's 4 dimensions → v2's 5-level hierarchy: Domain (L1) →
Subdomain (L2) → Concept Group (L3) → Canonical Concept (L4) →
Knowledge Object (L5).
Rationale: Two additional levels (Concept Groups and Knowledge Objects)
bridge the gap between abstract taxonomy domains and concrete documents.
Enables precise classification and retrieval.

TD-002: 100+ Concept Groups
------------------------------
Decision: 100+ concept groups across all 10 domains, each with code,

Rationale: Concept groups are the granular unit for Graph RAG sub-graph

TD-003: Canonical Concept Mapping
-------------------------------------
Decision: Level 4 maps directly to canonical-concepts.md with "Direct"
and "Related" mapping types.
Rationale: Not all concept groups have a dedicated canonical concept.
Direct mapping means the concept fully covers the group; Related means
partial coverage.

9. SEMANTIC QUALITY DECISIONS
================================================================================

SQD-001: 5-Dimension Quality Model
-------------------------------------
Decision: Quality = (Completeness × 0.25) + (Consistency × 0.25) +
(TranslationAccuracy × 0.20) + (DuplicateScore × 0.15) +
(AmbiguityScore × 0.15)
Rationale: Completeness and consistency are most important (50% combined).
Translation accuracy weighted separately for bilingual quality. Duplicate
and ambiguity detection prevent semantic conflicts.

SQD-002: 4 Quality Gates
----------------------------
Decision: Ingestion (automated), Review (human), Publication (score ≥0.80),
Audit (quarterly).
Rationale: Multi-layer validation without blocking ingestion. Automated
gates catch format issues. Human gates catch semantic issues. Quarterly
audits catch drift.

SQD-003: Continuous Monitoring
---------------------------------
Decision: Monthly automated scans, quarterly human audits, annual review.
Rationale: Semantic quality degrades over time as new terms are added,
standards evolve, and language usage changes. Proactive monitoring
prevents accumulation of low-quality entries.

12. RISKS AND FUTURE CONSIDERATIONS
================================================================================

RISK-01: Semantic Layer Scope Creep
---------------------------------------
The 10 documents cover vocabulary (145 terms), synonyms (68 groups),
bilingual lexicon (581 entries), acronyms (95 entries), units (100+
entries), rules, governance, quality, and taxonomy v2. Maintaining all
of these as the knowledge base grows will require significant ongoing
effort.
Mitigation: Most updates are automated (acronym extraction, unit parsing).
Human effort focused on vocabulary and synonym governance.

RISK-02: Persian Language Processing Challenges
--------------------------------------------------
Persian NLP tools for stemming, fuzzy matching, and embedding are less
mature than English. Persian character normalization (alef variants, ye
variants) is complex.
Mitigation: Character normalization rules documented in semantic-rules.md.
Multi-lingual embedding models (E5, LaBSE) evaluated for FA/EN pair quality.

RISK-03: Matching Hierarchy Performance
------------------------------------------
The 6-tier matching hierarchy (exact → acronym → synonym → stemmed →
fuzzy → embedding) must be efficient at scale. Embedding-based matching
is computationally expensive.
Mitigation: Early termination at exact/acronym/synonym match (covers
~90% of real-world queries). Embedding matching only as fallback.

RISK-04: Taxonomy v2 Maintenance
-----------------------------------
Taxonomy v2 adds 100+ concept groups and 6 new subdomains. This is
significantly more granular than v1 and may require frequent updates.
Mitigation: Concept groups are documented as living artifacts. Annual
review cycle with domain experts.

RISK-05: Iranian Terminology Divergence
------------------------------------------
Iranian engineering terminology diverges from international standards
in several areas (voltage levels, equipment naming, tariff structure).
The semantic layer must maintain both mappings.
Mitigation: Dedicated Iranian sections in every document. Where divergence
exists, both forms are tracked with context rules.

RISK-06: Translation Quality at Scale
----------------------------------------
The 581 bilingual entries require high-quality translation. Machine
translation may introduce errors, especially for technical terms.
Mitigation: All translations reviewed by domain expert before publication.
Quality gate at 0.80 minimum for bilingual entries.
